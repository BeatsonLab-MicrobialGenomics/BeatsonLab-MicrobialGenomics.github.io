Mitchell Stanton-Cook

Mitchell’s studies at the University of Queensland resulted in a Bachelor of Science (Bioinformatics) and a Honours degree (Biochemistry). Mitchell’s spent time in the lab of Dr Thomas Huber based at the Australian National University. Here he looked at computing protein interactions and dynamics from paramagnetic nuclear magnetic resonance data. Mitchell has since moved from a niche field in structural bioinformatics to the exciting realm of sequence bioinformatics. At present, he’s the groups system administrator/programmer and manages our next-generation sequencing (ngs) data sets. His work involves developing solutions for the efficient storage, processing and analysis of ngs data sets.

He loves linux, writes a fair bit of Python code and can handle himself in a high performance computing environment.

Mitchell is a Software Carpentry (SWC) Instructor. He's leading the a workshop in July.

In 2015 he'll be helping to organise HealthHack in Brisbane.

You can find Mitchell's publication record here. He also spends a fair bit of time on twitter and has a personal blog.
